# Tips for DSA Interviews

- **Always write pseudocode first:** Before jumping into the actual implementation, take a few minutes to write down the logic in plain English or pseudocode. This helps clarify your thought process, makes it easier to spot logic errors early, and gives the interviewer a clear understanding of your approach before you get bogged down in syntax.

- **Analyze complexity from pseudocode:** Before writing the actual code, analyze the time and space complexity based on your pseudocode. This allows you to catch inefficiencies early and discuss potential optimizations with the interviewer before committing to a specific implementation.

- **Confirm before coding:** Always check in with the interviewer to ensure they are satisfied with your current approach before you start writing the actual code. This ensures you're on the right track and saves time from implementing a suboptimal solution.
